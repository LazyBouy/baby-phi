## Concept alignment

- **Concept claim (verbatim or close paraphrase)**: The Permission Check engine gates access at resource boundaries; a Denied Decision refuses the action. Session launch is such a boundary; agents without the requisite grants on session actions must not launch.
- **Contradiction**: **Contradicts concept directly.** Shipped code logs `"advisory at M5; not blocking"` at [`launch.rs:221`](../../../../../../modules/crates/server/src/platform/sessions/launch.rs#L221) and proceeds to spawn the replay task regardless of step-1-to-6 denial. Only Step 0 (Catalogue) is actually gated.
- **Classification**: `contradicts-concept`
- **phi-core leverage status**: `N/A — no phi-core overlap` (permission-check engine is baby-phi-native)

## Plan vs. reality
- **Plan said** (plan-archive line 1246, §Part 4 P4 §launch_session Step 3): *"Run Permission Check steps 0–6."* — implying Denied at any step returns `PERMISSION_CHECK_FAILED_AT_STEP_N` (403).
- **Reality (shipped state at current HEAD)**: Launch's synthetic manifest (`actions=["launch_session"]`, `resource=["session"]`) doesn't match any grant Template A actually issues (Template A mints `[read, inspect, list]` on `project:<id>`, not `launch_session` on `session`). Strictly gating every launch would reject every M5 launch. Steps 1–6 surface `Decision` on `LaunchReceipt` + the preview endpoint but do NOT refuse. See [`launch.rs:174-221`](../../../../../../modules/crates/server/src/platform/sessions/launch.rs#L174-L221).
- **Root cause**: `cascading-upstream-deferral` (per-action manifest catalogue + real agent_loop both deferred; advisory Decision is the least-bad stopgap).
